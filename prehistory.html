Prehistory


The region of modern-day Palestine has an incredibly ancient history of human life, with remains dating back 1.5 million years. These early traces, found in Ubeidiya near the Sea of Galilee, show that Homo erectus—the ancestors of modern humans—passed through this area after migrating from Africa. Later, the Natufian culture, flourishing around 12,800–10,300 BCE, left signs of a more developed society, including tools, burial sites, and stone structures. This culture even coexisted with Neanderthals, who lived there from about 200,000 to 45,000 years ago.

Between 10,000 and 5000 BCE, early farming communities appeared, especially around Jericho. These early inhabitants built walls, shrines, and a 23-foot tower, making Jericho one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the world. By around 5000 BCE, new settlers arrived from Syria, bringing copper tools and creating more organized settlements that laid foundations for urban life in the area.
